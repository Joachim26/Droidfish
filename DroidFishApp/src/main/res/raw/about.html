About

Beth Harmon, a Special Edition of the Black Diamond Chess app, a fork of the great Android chess app named Droidfish developed by Peter Österlund. Black Diamond is a feature-rich graphical chess and is combined here with a 22 engines, It also includes some micro millisecond time controls and a sleep setting to enhance the human playability fun factor. Enjoy!

Engines:

Black Diamond, based on the great problem solver Stockfish clone Crystal issued by my good friend Joe Ellis from time to time.

Bluefish, another Stockfish clone, originally issued by my good friend Eelco de Groot, in classical search mode, places a ~22 percent greater emphasis on positional evaluation functions by reducing piece values.

In addition, Honey and Oki Maguro, are also included. Honey uses classical evaluation less often than current-dev-Stockfish and Oki Maguro is very close to current-dev-Stockfish and is named in honor of our friends in Japan, who first introduced Neural Net learning in Stockfish.

Oki Maguro translates to Big Tuna in Japanese. Neural Net learning increased Stockfish’s Elo rating by 150 Elo in a two-month span, enabling Stockfish to solidified its reputation as the strongest freely available chess engine in the world.

Also, I call out and tip my hat to the very creative and hardworking Stockfish Authors team. They epitomize the benefits of open source software and the quality that can be achieved by collaboration from the hundreds of developers around the world who have contributed. Also included is the great Stockfish fork, Cfish by Ronald De Mann and Stockfish 6, the first Stockfish release which supported syzygy tablebases (also developed by Ronald De Mann ).

The Stockfish Black Diamond Neural Network engines are paired with the acclaimed neural network Night Nurse 0.3 by Dietrich Kappe. This is the most appealing net for the human playing experience. For club players, set the base time control to 500 milliseconds (default) and the increment to 10 milliseconds (default). If you find it too easy, slowly increase the base and increment. To avoid instant move use the Engine Sleep function (default on) to put the engine to sleep anywhere from 1 second to 1 minute. You can also use UCI Limit Strength if the lowest time control settings are too strong.

Previously, Ethereal 2.75 was added, perhaps no other engine ever climbed the Elo ladder faster than Ethereal. In just a little over 5 years, it has reached the number two ranking of Open Source engines behind Stockfish. Great work by Andy Grant and his team of contributors.

Also included are three strong and stable engines: Andscacs v.0921 by Daniel José Queraltó, and Fruit 2.1 and Senpai 1.0, both by Fabien Letouzey.

Shallow Blue 2.0, by Rhys Rustad-Elliott, is also included. A rock solid stable engine that may play close to FIDE 1850 on an Android device. A very nice engine for humans to play.

Included previously is the transformational chess engine Glaurung 2.2 written by Tord Romstad, Tord now works with Grandmaster World Champion Magnus Carlsen and Carsen's PlayMagnus app and website. It was transformational as it was released open source and it was one of the strongest engines in the world. It directly led to the creation of Stockfish, the current strongest chess engine in the world, with its hundreds of collaborators and authors. Glaurung 2.2 has many UCI evaluation and search options which the user can customize to his satisfaction for interesting and fun play.

Previously, CT800 1.41 by Rasmus Althoff was added. CT800 is one of my all-time favorite engines to play against. Also included is Demolito, written by Lucas Braesch, a very strong engine, probably slightly stronger than Rybka 4.1 in strength.

Also included previously, were the engines Defenchess 2.2 by Can Cetin and Dogac Eldenk, Laser 1.7 by the brothers Jeffrey & Michael An and Xiphos 0.6 by Milos Tatarevic. All three of these engines are top tier open source engines with respect to their strength and reliability.

We are adding the Stockfish clone Corchess by Ivan Ivec in this release . Ivan is known for his high level of play on ICCF and Corchess mirrors his level of play. Ivan finished in 3rd place in the World Cup 21 Finals, while earning his ICCF International Master Title.

We added the Stockfish clone MOJO by Jörg Oster previously. Jörg is known for his modifications that help reduce Stockfish's occasional chess blindness.

RubiChess by Andreas Matthies was also added previously and it is known for its aggressive style of play.

In a previous releases, we added a two new color themes Green and Night. The Green theme is based on the ubiquitous vinyl Green rolled up chess boards sold and in use throughout the world. The Night color theme is for those situations where you want to keep the light down — particularly at night. Previously introduced were the very fast time controls, now in Milliseconds and a new setting called Engine Sleep — used to put the engine to sleep for a few seconds before making its move. A perfect combination to weaken the engine naturally while not having it move instantly. I always had appreciated the ability to ponder while the engine is on the clock.

We added the new chess engine Beth Harmon, based on Stockfish in this version. Beth Harmon values pieces more than postion evaulations when not in NNUE mode. In addition some of the paths for syzygy and NNUE networks are now easier to modify. We added a new option to Disable the 50 move rule when using syzygy tablebases. Only applies when 7 or less men the board.

BlackDiamond also works on the Amazon Fire Stick where you can play on the BIG screen. A HUGE call out to Al Scally for his assistance on the Fire Stick deployment! You will need the app Toggle Mouse or something similiar to select the menu options and move the pieces.

Features

  • Opening book
  • Unlimited undo/redo
  • Copy/paste to/from clipboard
  • Analysis mode
  • Two player mode
  • Computer vs computer mode
  • Setup positions
  • Extensive PGN support
  • Adjustable playing strength
  • Third party UCI engines
  • Gaviota endgame tablebases
  • Syzygy endgame tablebases

Hints

  • Long press mode/left/right buttons for additional commands.
  • When re-playing a game, change game mode to Edit/re-play Game to be able to undo/redo single half moves.

Opening books

DroidFish contains a small internal opening book. If you want to use a larger book, you can configure DroidFish to use external polyglot or CTG book files:

  1. Copy one or more polyglot book files to the DroidFish/book directory on the SD card. Polyglot books must have the file extension .bin. Note! The Android file system is case sensitive, so the extension must be .bin, not .Bin or .BIN.
  2. Copy one or more CTG book files to the DroidFish/book directory. A CTG book consists of three files with file extensions .ctg, .ctb and .cto. You must copy all three files.
  3. From the program menu, activate the Select opening book function.
  4. Select the opening book file you want to use.

PGN files

DroidFish can load games from PGN files.

  1. Copy one or more PGN files to the DroidFish/pgn directory on the SD card.
  2. Long press the chess board and select Load game from PGN file.
  3. Select the file, then the game within the file you want to load.

To edit game headers such as player names, long press the move list and select Edit Headers. To edit comments for a move, long press the move list and select Edit Comments.

To save your edited game, long press the chess board and select Save game to PGN file. Select the file to save to, then select the position in the file where the new game will be saved.

Note! If the OI File Manager is installed, any directory can be used to load/save games.

To add computer analysis to the game tree, long press the analysis information when the computer is thinking and select Add Analysis. The current principal variation is added as a variation in the game tree.

In analysis mode, it is possible to add null moves to the game tree. Be aware though that null moves are not included in the PGN standard and may not work in other PGN reading chess programs.

Scid files

If you have Scid on the go installed, you can read Scid database files directly from DroidFish. Long press the chess board and select Load game from Scid file.

FEN/EPD files

DroidFish can load positions from FEN/EPD files.

  1. Copy one or more FEN/EPD files to the DroidFish/epd directory on the SD card.
  2. Long press the chess board and select Load position from file.
  3. Select the file, then the position within the file you want to load.

You can also load positions from external programs, such as ChessOcr (non-free), which will let you use the camera to take a picture of a chess diagram and automatically set up the position in DroidFish.

  1. Long press the chess board and select Retrieve Position.
  2. Follow the instructions in the external program.

UCI engines

DroidFish can use third party UCI engines.

  1. Copy one or more UCI engine binaries to the DroidFish/uci directory on the SD card. Note! The binaries must be compiled for Android.
  2. From the program menu, choose Manage Chess Engines and Select Chess Engine and select the engine to use.

UCI engines on a remote server

DroidFish can use UCI engines that run on a remote server.

  1. Install chess network server software on the remote computer.
    1. For windows, install server software from the DroidFish page.
    2. For linux, mini-inetd from the tcputils package can be used.
  2. Select Manage Chess Engines in the program menu, create a new network engine and enter the host name and port number for the remote engine.
  3. From the program menu, choose Manage Chess Engines and Select Chess Engine and select the engine to use.

Gaviota endgame tablebases

To use Gaviota endgame tablebases:

  1. Copy .gtb.cp4 files to the DroidFish/gtb directory on the SD card.
  2. Use the Endgame Tablebases preferences screen to control how the tablebases are used.

Syzygy endgame tablebases

To use Syzygy endgame tablebases:

  1. Copy .rtbw and optionally .rtbz files to the DroidFish/rtb directory on the SD card.
  2. Use the Endgame Tablebases preferences screen to control how the tablebases are used.
